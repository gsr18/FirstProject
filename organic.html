Organic Farming & The Sustainable Approach

What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is a method of farming that relies on natural processes and avoids the use of synthetic chemicals, pesticides, and fertilizers. It nurtures soil health through crop rotation, composting, and other regenerative practices. By focusing on a balanced ecosystem, organic farming promotes biodiversity and ensures food that is free from harmful residues.

Benefits of Organic Farming

  • Environmental Protection: Organic farming reduces pollution, conserves water, reduces soil erosion, and increases soil fertility.
  • Better Soil Health: Techniques like crop rotation and composting promote nutrient-rich and resilient soil.
  • Healthier Produce: Organic farming results in crops that are free from synthetic pesticides, providing healthier food for consumers.
  • Promotes Biodiversity: Organic farms support a wider range of plants, animals, and insects, which help to balance the ecosystem.
